All About Identity Theft

Identity theft is the crime of using someone's personal identifying information, such as their Social Security number, name, or credit card number, without permission to commit fraud or other offenses. A person's identity can be stolen through a variety of methods, both online and offline, including data breaches, phishing, and physical theft.

Common Types of Identity Theft

  • Financial Identity Theft: This is the most common form, where a criminal uses your information to open new credit accounts, make unauthorized purchases, or steal money from your existing accounts.
  • Tax Identity Theft: A thief uses your stolen Social Security number and other personal data to file a fraudulent tax return and claim your refund.
  • Medical Identity Theft: A criminal uses your health insurance information to receive medical care, fill prescriptions, or make fraudulent insurance claims.
  • Criminal Identity Theft: This occurs when a criminal provides your name and other identifiers to law enforcement during an arrest to evade prosecution or hide their true identity.
  • Child Identity Theft: Because children do not typically have credit reports, a fraudster can steal a child's Social Security number and use it for years to open accounts undetected.
  • Synthetic Identity Theft: This involves combining real information (like a Social Security number) with fake information (like a made-up name) to create a new fraudulent identity.

How to Prevent Identity Theft

  • Use Strong Passwords: Use strong passwords and multi-factor authentication for your online accounts. Avoid using the same password for multiple services.
  • Be Careful with Personal Information: Do not give out sensitive information over the phone or by email unless you initiated the contact.
  • Shred Sensitive Documents: Shred documents before throwing them away, such as credit card offers and bank statements.
  • Limit Your Social Media Presence: Be cautious about how much personal information you share online.
  • Use Secure Wi-Fi Connections: Avoid performing financial transactions on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks.
  • Monitor Your Financial Activity: Regularly review your credit card and bank statements for unauthorized charges.
  • Consider a Credit Freeze: A credit freeze restricts access to your credit report, making it difficult for an identity thief to open new accounts.

How to Report Identity Theft

  • File a Report with the FTC: In the U.S., go to IdentityTheft.gov, which will provide you with a personalized recovery plan.
  • Contact Companies Involved: Inform your bank, credit card issuers, and other affected businesses about the fraudulent activity.
  • Place a Fraud Alert or Credit Freeze: Contact one of the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion) to place an alert on your credit report.
  • File a Police Report: This can be helpful for disputing fraudulent accounts or charges.
  • Change Your Passwords: Update the login credentials for all compromised accounts immediately.
