Essential Online Safety Practices

Follow these crucial practices, compiled from our guides, to significantly boost your online security.

Think Before You Act

Be skeptical of unsolicited messages. If an email or text creates a sense of urgency, fear, or offers something too good to be true, pause and verify its legitimacy before clicking any links or opening attachments.

Use Strong Passwords & Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Create complex, unique passwords for each of your accounts. More importantly, enable MFA (or 2FA) whenever possible. It adds a critical second layer of security that protects you even if your password is stolen.

Keep Your Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, web browser, and other applications. These updates often contain vital security patches that fix vulnerabilities exploited by malware and ransomware.

Back Up Your Important Data

Maintain regular backups of your important files on an external drive or a secure cloud service. Keep backups offline and disconnected from your network to protect them from ransomware attacks.

Monitor Your Accounts

Frequently review your bank, credit card, and credit reports for any suspicious activity. The sooner you spot fraudulent charges or accounts, the easier it is to resolve the issue.

Limit Your Online Footprint

Be cautious about the personal information you share on social media and other public platforms. Criminals can use these details to craft convincing social engineering attacks or to commit identity theft.
