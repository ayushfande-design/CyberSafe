All About Phishing Scams

Phishing is a type of cybercrime that involves impersonating a trustworthy entity to trick people into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card details, or bank account information. Attackers use deceptive emails, text messages, fake websites, or even phone calls to execute these social engineering attacks, ultimately aiming to steal your identity, money, or install malware on your devices.

How Phishing Works

Impersonation: The attacker sends a message (email, text, social media) that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank, a reputable company, or even a known contact.

Deception: The message often contains a sense of urgency or an enticing offer, designed to make you act quickly without thinking.

Malicious Links/Attachments: The message will usually contain a link to a fake website or an attachment that, when clicked, will either:

  • Capture Information: Lead you to a fraudulent website that mimics a real one, where you are prompted to enter personal data.
  • Install Malware: Download malicious software (malware) onto your device, which can steal your information or compromise your system.

Common Phishing Tactics

  • Urgency: Messages claiming there is a problem with your account that needs immediate attention.
  • Attractive Offers: "Too good to be true" deals or claims of winning a prize.
  • Targeted Attacks: Spear phishing involves customized attacks that use information about the target to make the scam more convincing.
  • Vishing and Smishing: Phishing attacks conducted via voice calls (vishing) or SMS text messages (smishing).

How to Protect Yourself

  • Be Skeptical: Always question suspicious emails and messages, especially if they seem too good to be true or create a sense of urgency.
  • Verify the Source: Look for inconsistencies in email addresses or sender information.
  • Do Not Click on Suspicious Links: Go to the company's official website directly instead of clicking on links in suspicious messages.
  • Use Strong Security: Enable two-factor authentication and use reputable antivirus software.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: Report phishing attempts to your email provider and relevant cybersecurity organizations.
